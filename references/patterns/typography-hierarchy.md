Typographic Hierarchy is the reference for the typography classes used across the FlowX designer and the rules for composing them into pages. It defines four text roles. Page Title (class "xxl bold", matching the xxl-bold text style preset) is 24px with a 38px line height at weight 700 in neutral-900 (var(--flowx-neutrals-900)); it is the single page-level heading in the top title card, and there is exactly one per page. Section Title (class "p3 bold", matching l-bold) is 18px/28px at weight 700 in neutral-900; it titles a major section inside the body card, such as Filters, Entries, or General Settings. Subsection Title (class "p1 bold", matching m-bold) is 14px/24px at weight 700 in neutral-900; it titles a labelled subsection or grouping inside a section. Description (class "caption regular", matching s-regular) is 12px/16px at weight 400 in neutral-500 (var(--flowx-neutrals-500)); it is short supporting copy paired with any title. The Description style is identical at every level; the surrounding title is what sets the hierarchy. The composition examples also use a field label style of 14px/20px at weight 600 in neutral-900 for form field labels, though it is not one of the four documented roles.

The combining rules are: every page uses one Page Title, sections sit below it, subsections group fields inside a section, and any title may be paired with a Description when extra context is needed. Titles and their descriptions are stacked with a 6px gap.

Compositions are built from bordered cards: 1px solid neutral-100 (var(--flowx-neutrals-100)) border, 12px corner radius, white (#ffffff) background, 24px padding, sitting on a neutral-50 (var(--flowx-neutrals-50)) outer background. Inside a card, the title-plus-description block is followed by content with a 16px gap. Stacked cards are separated by 16px. Form fields place the label 6px above the control. Action buttons (Cancel as a bordered secondary button with blue-500 text, Save as a filled primary button in var(--flowx-blue-500)) sit right-aligned with an 8px gap at the bottom of the card that owns the form.

Three composition scenarios are documented. Scenario A is one section with a subsection inside: a page header card, then a section card nesting a subsection card that holds the form fields and the button row. Scenario B is sibling section cards directly under the page title: each section gets its own bordered card, and the button row lives in the last one. Scenario C is sibling sections sharing the same card: a single bordered card contains multiple Section Title blocks stacked with 24px gaps and one button row at the bottom of the shared card. Note: the bordered 12px-radius card shell described here is how the hierarchy page illustrates its examples; on real screens, card containers follow the Cards pattern (16px radius, Primary shadow or Secondary border) and this pattern governs only the text roles and spacing rules.
