The FlowX card model has exactly two families. Primary cards structure a page and Secondary cards group content inside a Primary. Every card in both families shares a 16px corner radius, uses the FlowX font (var(--font-flowx), Open Sans), lays out its children as a column flex stack, and applies its padding equally on both axes; the gap value is the spacing between the card's stacked children. Primary cards sit directly on the grey app page background, which is neutrals-50 (#f7f8f9). The difference between the families is shadow versus border, plus the padding and gap step.

A Primary card has a white (#ffffff) surface, the shadow 2px 2px 24px rgba(22,52,98,.08), no border, 16px radius, 24px padding on both axes, and a 16px gap. Titles and icons are optional: a Primary card can lead with an icon tile and title, carry a title alone, or have neither. Full-bleed content such as a table can drop the padding and run edge to edge while staying clipped to the card's 16px radius; in that mode the table itself is the Primary card (white surface, the same shadow, 16px radius, overflow hidden, no card padding).

A Secondary card has a transparent or white surface, no shadow or elevation, a 1px solid neutrals-100 (#e3e8ed) border, and a 16px radius. It comes in three sizes that differ only by padding and gap: Large is 24px padding with a 16px gap, Medium is 16px padding with an 8px gap, and Small is 8px padding with a 4px gap. Large is used for subsections like a confusion matrix or training distribution, the protocol, authorization and general settings subsections of an external file-storage data source, and the evaluators and scorers in the Eval Definitions tab. Medium is used for score cards (label, percentage and progress bar) inside the experiment report when comparing two experiments in Evals. Small is used for Improvement Suggestions rows on the experiment page. Titles and icons are optional here too: a card can pair an icon with a title, carry only a label, or have neither.

Typography inside cards, in the light theme the specimens document: a large page-lead title is 24px weight 700 with a 38px line-height in neutrals-900 (#1d232c); a standard card title is 18px weight 700 with a 28px line-height in neutrals-900; description text is 12px regular with a 16px line-height in neutrals-500 (#64748b); small body rows are 12px with an 18px line-height in neutrals-500, with lead words bolded (weight 700) in neutrals-900; uppercase micro-labels are 11px weight 600 with 0.04em letter spacing in neutrals-400 (#8390a2); big stats are 24px weight 700 with tabular numerals. Common inner elements include the white DS button (36px tall, 6px 12px padding, 8px radius, 1px #e3e8ed border, 14px weight-600 blue-500 #006bd8 text), icon tiles (54px or 48px square, 8px radius, neutrals-50 #f7f8f9 fill), pill-radius progress bars with a neutrals-100 #e3e8ed track and blue-400 #3389e0, green-500 #008060 or red-500 #e62200 fills, status pills (4px 12px padding, 999 radius, neutrals-50 fill, 1px neutrals-100 border, 6px tone dot in green-500/orange-500 #fd6b1c/red-500, value text in green-700 #005b44, orange-700 #b44c14 or red-600 #d11f00), 1px #e3e8ed inner dividers, and the full-bleed entity table grid (0.5px #cbd1db row borders, 50px header, 56px rows, 14px horizontal cell padding, 12px weight-600 #64748b headers, 14px #0f1114 cells).

Guidelines: pick the family by where the card sits, Primary on the grey page and Secondary inside a Primary, then read off its spec. Every card uses a 16px radius with no exceptions. Primary means white plus shadow and no border; Secondary means a 1px neutral-100 border with no shadow. Padding is equal on both axes and the three Secondary sizes differ only by padding and gap (L 24/16, M 16/8, S 8/4). Titles and icons are optional in both families, and full-bleed content may drop the Primary padding and run edge to edge, clipped to the radius. Dark-theme card values are not documented; the pattern page keeps its example mockups light.
