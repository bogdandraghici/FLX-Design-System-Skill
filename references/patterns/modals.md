Modals is a product-level pattern (not a registered base component): an overlay dialog for short tasks, confirmations, or focused content. It accepts the following props: size ("XS", "S", "M", or "L", defaulting to "M"), inverted (boolean, default false), showSubtitle (boolean, default true), step ("single" or "multi", default "single"), title (string), and subtitle (string). Widths per size are XS 400px, S 600px, M 800px, and L 1426px (documented as 80% of screen width). The container is a vertical auto-layout surface with a 16px corner radius, a drop shadow of 2px 2px 24px rgba(22, 52, 98, 0.12), 32px padding and a 32px gap between sections at S/M/L, reduced to 24px padding and gap at XS. The background is #ffffff (grays/50) in light mode and #1d232c (neutrals/900) in inverted/dark mode.

The anatomy from top to bottom: a centered header stack (4px gap, fills width) holding the title and optional subtitle; a content slot where the calling context injects its own body such as forms, text, lists, tables, or media; and a horizontal action bar at the bottom. The title is Open Sans Bold (700) at 24px with a 38px line-height, colored #2a313a (neutrals/800) in light and #ffffff in dark. The subtitle is Open Sans Regular (400) at 12px/16px, colored #64748b in light and #8390a2 (neutrals/400) in dark, and can be hidden via showSubtitle. A 24x24 X close icon is absolutely positioned in the top-right corner (inset by the container padding) and is always rendered; its fill is #64748b in both themes. In multi-step modals a 24x24 CaretLeft back icon appears in the top-left corner, also #64748b. The Figma spec's content placeholder is a horizontal, centered, fill-width slot with 10px padding, 10px gap, 16px corner radius, and 196px height, filled #f0ecfa (helpers/100) with a #7a4ed1 (helpers/500) label in light, and #7a4ed1 at 20% opacity with an #f0ecfa label in dark.

The action bar fills the width, hugs its height, uses a 16px gap between buttons, and is right-aligned. Buttons have an 8px corner radius and Open Sans SemiBold (600) 14px/24px text. In light mode the secondary (Cancel) button is #ffffff with a 1px #e3e8ed stroke and #006bd8 text, and the primary button is #006bd8 fill with #ffffff text. In dark mode the secondary button is transparent with a 1px #5b6a7e stroke and #3389e0 text, and the primary is #3389e0 fill with #ffffff text. Button placement rules: with 2 buttons, they together fill the right 50% of the footer (25% each); with 1 button, it takes the right 25% or hugs its content; with 3 buttons, a tertiary text button sits on the left and secondary + primary sit on the right at 25% each. The left half of the footer is reserved for optional info text such as "Models enabled: 3/12". Exception: in XS modals the buttons stretch to fill 100% of the row (1 button 100%, 2 at 50% each, 3 at 33% each). The primary action is always right-most and never destructive-on-the-right; the secondary sits to its left.

For multi-step modals, replace Cancel with Back (primary becomes Next) and add the back icon in the top-left corner; the X close icon stays in the top-right and exits the entire flow. Never stack a modal on another modal; replace the current modal's content instead. Usage guidance: reserve modals for tasks needing full attention, keep titles short and action-oriented, and pick the smallest size that comfortably fits, most modals should be Medium or smaller. XS suits small in-context pop-overs that don't need an overlay; L is for genuinely wide or complex content such as tables or merge-conflict resolution. Form bodies prefer M or L. Always give at least one explicit dismissal (X icon, Cancel, or Escape). Let overflowing bodies scroll internally rather than growing taller than the viewport. Accessibility: role="dialog" (or "alertdialog" for destructive confirmations), aria-modal="true", aria-labelledby on the title, aria-describedby on the subtitle or body when useful; trap Tab focus inside, dismiss on Escape, move focus to the first interactive element on open, and restore focus to the trigger on close. Related components: button, input-field, select-field.

Values not specified in the sources: the overlay/scrim color and opacity, and button height/padding (the docs preview renders buttons with 6px vertical and 12px horizontal padding, but that value is illustrative, not from the Figma spec).
